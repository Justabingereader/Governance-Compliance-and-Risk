# Information Security Management Strategy

## Executive Summary

This document is a board-level Information Security Management (ISM) Strategy, written from the perspective of a senior security leader establishing the security posture and governance foundations for an organisation. It is the kind of document a CISO would present to executive leadership and the board to secure buy-in and set the direction for an organisation's security programme.

The strategy covers: vision and strategic objectives, governance structure and leadership accountability, risk identification and treatment methodology, framework selection and alignment to security standards, a prioritised action plan with timetable, technical/physical/administrative controls, security automation, incident management, KPIs and reporting structure, budget requirements, and an annual review process.

The overarching goal is to protect critical information assets, ensure regulatory compliance, and align security investment directly with business continuity and growth objectives — framing security not as a cost centre, but as an enabler of organisational resilience.

**Key skills demonstrated:** ISM strategy development, executive communication, governance and leadership alignment, risk management frameworks, KPI design, security control selection, budget justification, and long-form strategic document writing.
